Attune is an app that leads school kids to find validation and deeper understanding through their emotions.

Exploration and Analysis

We began our brainstorming process by reaching out to middle and high school teachers, students, and parents to hear from them what they thought of certain aspects of classroom culture. From there we crafted personas for the purpose of exploring the classroom from their point of view.

There were three main things that were very influential to our topic from the get-go

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)

CBT is a type of talk therapy that guides participants to identify thought patterns that are potentially destructive

During CBT, participants are encouraged to think outside of their immediate train of thought through robust ‚ÄúSocratic‚Äù questioning such as: Are there long-term effects of feeling this way? Are my thoughts on this situation accurate? Am I basing my thoughts on facts or feelings?

Robert Plutchik‚Äôs Wheel of Emotions

A categorization of 8 base emotions on an axis of similarities, opposites, and combination, as well as intensity. The logic of the wheel is derived from survival mechanisms and evolutionary history, with acknowledgment of the hypothetical constructs of emotion.

While we eventually decided that a literal translation of the wheel was too esoteric, this got us thinking about why categorizing one's emotions is so important

Mindfulness

An eastern philosophy found mostly in Buddhism and Hinduism, which in western practice uses intuitive reflection on one‚Äôs emotions and experiences. It is centered around the idea that the individual will begin to extract their subjective experience and objectify it, realizing that their emotions do not define them and that they can be in control of how they feel, through the simple act of understanding.

We were particularly excited by the overlap between the wheel and mindfulness, kind of creating this interactive approach to exploring oneself, with the potential for CBT to guide and eventually extend the scope of that exploration.
