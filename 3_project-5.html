Indentity, Editorial Typography


Though I guess this style can hardly be evalutated as "editorial." Maybe anti-editorial would be more accurate.

Beginning with a category and a statement of intent, to a logo, then to the spread design, this acedemic project became very dense.

To consolodate, most of my iterations are contained in gifs.



If you're interested in the initial sketches, or an in-depth statement of my philosphy and voice behind the final and runner-up concepts, pull up this process document here.

Scroll on for pretty pictures.

The ultimate sentiment of the publication is: we do what we want, we do crack, we do anarchy. Alternatively: we came, we saw, we anarchy.

And also we steal articles from The Onion. But this is an acedemic project so that's okay.







Contained in this article: An editor's note by Ernest Hemmingway, An overview of the Tennets of Anarchism by Stephen King, a brief analysis of Rumpelstilkskin by the esteemed William Shakespeare, an interview with your mom by F. Scott Fitzgerald, and concluding with a groundbreaking study on the positive effects of sniffing glue in adolescents.

I visualized my anarcho-punk muse with dense textures formed with a typographical tribute to my home boy David Carson, and smearing actual dirt all over everything.
