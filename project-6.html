I analyzed and user tested craigslist, then redesigned it. This is the Craislist we know and love, but ~better~

Initial Digital Sketching

Current UI vs My Prototype

The home screen had a very homogenous texture, it was difficult primarily regarding the hierarchy to discern which elements were important where to find them.

In my redesign, I took their logical arrangement of the categories as well as their easy viewing of all elements of all categories, and simply made it more – pretty! With plenty of serene whitespace and a clear hierarchy.

The first thumbnail listing of the housing has little to no breakdown of elements into categories, instead opting to throw all the filter options at the user to sift through.

My solution differentiated the finer detail into dropdown categories, with the most important non-categorized elements as easily accessible filters.

When you expand a listing, the workflow and presentation of the data is nonlinear and is difficult for my overworked, ADHD brain to comprehend where I should logically look first to find the button or function I am looking for.

In my redesign, immediate access widgets are available right next to the title of the listing, and the rest of the information is available where it would logically fall in a user’s thought process; such as the reply button being situated below all of the details.

When posting, the breadcrumbs are very nice, so I kept something similar, adding visual indication of the sections to come so there is less anticipation or impatience when going through the entire conditional positing process.

There is no type hierarchy in the fillable form, though it is logically arranged in the chronology of each section, so I left that alone and made the hierarchy stronger and more fitting to the aesthetics of my redesign.
